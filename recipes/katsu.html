Spicy Chicken Katsu

These sandwiches combine the shattering panko crispiness of katsu-style cutlets and the fiery heat found in Nashville-style hot chicken with some cooling shredded lettuce to put out the fire.

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup chopped bread-and-butter pickles
  • 2 Tbsp. plus 1/4 cup hot sauce, such as Crystal
  • 1 tsp. kosher salt, plus more
  • 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • 3 Tbsp. cayenne pepper
  • 2 tsp. garlic powder
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups panko (Japanese breadcrumbs)
  • 4 skinless, boneless chicken thighs, lightly pounded to 1/2" thick
  • 8 slices milk bread

Instructions:
  • Sauce:
    1. Combine matyonnaise, pickles and 2 Tbsp hot sauce in a small bowl. Season with salt; set aside.
  • Preparing the Chicken:
    1. Combine flour, cayenne, garlic powder, and remaining 1 tsp. salt in a medium bowl.
    2. Whisk egg and remaining 1/4 cup hot sauce in another medium bowl.
    3. Place panko on a large plate.
    4. Working one at a time, dip chicken into flour dredge, turning to coat.
    5. Shake off any excess
    6. Dip into egg mixture until coated, shaking off excess.
    7. Dredge in panko, turning to coat.
    8. Transfer to a large plate or small bowl
  • Cooking the Chicken:
    1. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high until shimmering.
    2. Working in 2 batches, cook chicken
      • Reduce heat to medium if it's browning too quickly
    3. Cook until goldfen and very crisp on both sides, about three minutes per side.
    4. Transfer to wire rack.
  • Plating:
    1. Spread reserved spicy mayo on one side of each slice of bread.
    2. Top with chicken and shredded lettuce.
    3. Close sandwich.
    4. Serve with plenty of dressing on the side.
